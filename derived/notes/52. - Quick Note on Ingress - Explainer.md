## What This Lesson Is Doing

The previous lesson explained why the course is not going with a plain `LoadBalancer` service.

This lesson introduces the alternative:

`Ingress`

At a high level, ingress accepts outside traffic and routes it to the right services inside the cluster.

## The Main Routing Idea

The ingress diagram shows the key benefit very clearly:

- one outside entry point
- multiple possible internal destinations

That is exactly why ingress fits this app better than a single load balancer service.

## The Big Warning

Most of this lesson is really a documentation warning.

The course says you must not confuse two similarly named projects:

- `kubernetes/ingress-nginx`
- `nginxinc/kubernetes-ingress`

They both sound like “NGINX ingress.”

They both have documentation that looks similar.

But they are not the same project.

The course is explicitly using:

`kubernetes/ingress-nginx`

That is the project the rest of the setup will refer to.

## Why This Matters

If you read the wrong docs, everything starts to feel inconsistent:

- different installation steps
- different configuration examples
- different assumptions

So the practical lesson here is:

before you do anything with ingress, make sure you are looking at the right project.

## Best Reuse
